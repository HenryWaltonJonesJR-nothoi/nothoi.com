nothoi

/ˈnō-thoy/ · noun, plural

Greek, νόθοι

1. Children born of mixed or uncertain origin; those who exist between recognized categories.

"Neither fully citizen nor foreigner, the nothoi occupied a liminal space in Athenian society."

contemporary usage

2. Hybrid thinking; intelligence that emerges from the collaboration between human cognition and artificial systems.

"The solution wasn't purely human intuition or machine logic—it was nothoi."

Origin: Ancient Greek nóthos (νόθος), meaning "bastard" or "of mixed origin." Reclaimed c. 2025 to describe the productive ambiguity of human-AI cognitive partnerships.
