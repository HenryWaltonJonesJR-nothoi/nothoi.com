Privacy Policy

Effective Date

March 7, 2026

Who We Are

Nothoi LLC ("we," "us," "our") operates LifeOS, a private AI assistant and notification service. This privacy policy describes how we collect, use, and protect information from users of our SMS messaging service.

Information We Collect

When you opt in to our SMS service, we collect your mobile phone number and the content of messages you send to our service number. We do not collect any other personal information through our SMS service.

How We Use Your Information

Your phone number is used solely to deliver LifeOS notifications, including system status alerts, daily briefings, and AI-generated conversational responses. Message content is processed to generate relevant responses and is stored on private infrastructure operated by Nothoi LLC.

How We Store Your Information

Your phone number and message history are stored in a secure, authenticated contacts file on private, self-hosted infrastructure operated by Nothoi LLC. Data is not stored in third-party cloud databases or customer relationship management systems. Access is restricted to the account owner.

Information Sharing

We will not sell, share, or disclose your mobile information to third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Your mobile information is never shared with or sold to third parties or lead generators. We may share information only if required by law or legal process.

Opt-Out and Deletion

You may opt out of SMS messages at any time by replying STOP to +1 (313) 800-0822. Upon opt-out, we will cease all messaging to your number. You may request complete deletion of your phone number and message history by contacting us at the address below. Deletion requests will be honored within 30 days.

Contact Us

For privacy inquiries or data deletion requests, contact Nothoi LLC at privacy@nothoi.com.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted at this URL with an updated effective date. Continued use of the service after changes constitutes acceptance.
